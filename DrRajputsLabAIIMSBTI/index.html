Our Focus Areas

Research Projects

Our lab investigates molecular mechanisms underlying cancer progression, metastasis, and novel therapeutic strategies using advanced cellular and molecular techniques.

🔬

ETS1 Regulation of EMT in Breast Cancer

Investigating how the transcription factor ETS1 orchestrates epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition pathways driving metastasis.

↻ Hover for details

ETS1 & EMT Regulation

We explore ETS1's role as a master regulator of EMT-related gene expression networks. Using ChIP-seq, CRISPR-Cas9 knockouts, and RNA-seq, we map transcriptional circuitries that enable cancer cells to acquire invasive phenotypes.

ChIP-seq CRISPR RNA-seq Metastasis
💊

Tadalafil Cytotoxicity in Breast Cancer

Evaluating the repurposed PDE5 inhibitor Tadalafil as a novel cytotoxic agent against triple-negative breast cancer cells.

↻ Hover for details

Drug Repurposing Strategy

Tadalafil, a FDA-approved PDE5 inhibitor, shows promising anti-proliferative activity. We characterize its mechanisms of cytotoxicity, cGMP pathway modulation, and synergistic combinations with existing chemotherapeutics in TNBC models.

PDE5 Inhibitor Drug Repurposing TNBC cGMP pathway
🩸

cfDNA/ctDNA Liquid Biopsy Studies

Developing minimally invasive liquid biopsy approaches using circulating free DNA and circulating tumor DNA as cancer biomarkers.

↻ Hover for details

Liquid Biopsy Diagnostics

We quantify and characterize cfDNA/ctDNA from cancer patient plasma to detect tumor-derived mutations, monitor treatment response, and develop early diagnostic panels using ddPCR and next-generation sequencing approaches.

ddPCR NGS Biomarkers Early Detection
🌱

Cancer Stem Cell Characterization

Identifying and targeting cancer stem cell populations responsible for tumor initiation, resistance, and recurrence.

↻ Hover for details

CSC Biology & Targeting

We use flow cytometry, sphere-forming assays, and single-cell RNA sequencing to identify CSC markers in breast and cervical cancer. Identifying vulnerabilities unique to CSCs guides development of stemness-targeting therapies.

Flow Cytometry scRNA-seq CD44/CD24 Stemness
🌐

Tumor Microenvironment & Immune Evasion

Understanding how cancer cells remodel their microenvironment to escape immune surveillance and promote progression.

↻ Hover for details

TME Immunobiology

Our team investigates cancer-associated fibroblasts, tumor-associated macrophages, and regulatory T cells in shaping immunosuppressive niches. We evaluate immunotherapy sensitization strategies using novel combination approaches.

CAFs TAMs Immunotherapy PD-L1
🧪

Epigenetic Regulation in Cancer

Mapping DNA methylation and histone modification landscapes that silence tumor suppressors and activate oncogenes.

↻ Hover for details

Epigenomics Approaches

Using ATAC-seq, bisulfite sequencing, and H3K27me3 ChIP-seq, we uncover epigenetic alterations driving transcriptional rewiring in cancer cells. Our goal is to identify druggable epigenetic vulnerabilities for targeted intervention.

ATAC-seq Methylation Histones DNMT inhibitors

Meet the Team

A passionate team of scientists, clinicians, and students united by the mission to decode cancer biology and translate discoveries into patient benefit.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Sanjay Kumar

MBBS, MD (Biochemistry) · Assistant Professor
Department of Biochemistry, AIIMS Bathinda

Dr. Sanjay Kumar leads the Cancer Biology Laboratory with a focus on molecular mechanisms of cancer progression, particularly ETS1-mediated EMT regulation, PDE5 inhibitor-based therapeutics, and liquid biopsy diagnostics. His translational research bridges fundamental molecular biology with clinical oncology to identify novel cancer biomarkers and therapeutic targets.
